# CODEBOOK: Thailand Human Rights Qualitative Analysis

This codebook provides the analytical framework for coding and analyzing expert interviews for the qualitative chapter of the Human Rights in Thailand report. The framework aligns with the WJP Rule of Law Index conceptual structure.

---

## DIMENSION 0: Current Status of Fundamental Rights in Thailand

*General assessment of the overall human rights landscape in Thailand, capturing cross-cutting themes and contextual factors.*

| Code | Sub-indicator | Description | Topics to Identify |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **0.1** | Overall Assessment | General perception of the state of fundamental rights | Overall evaluation, perceived trends, temporal comparisons |
| **0.2** | Key Challenges | Main challenges identified across dimensions | Systemic obstacles, structural problems, recurring issues |
| **0.3** | Areas of Progress | Recognized advances in fundamental rights | Recent improvements, positive reforms, institutional developments |
| **0.4** | Contextual Factors | Political, social, or historical factors influencing rights | Military coups, political transitions, regional context, COVID-19 impacts |

---

## DIMENSION 1: Civil and Political Rights

*Fundamental freedoms and rights that protect individuals from undue interference by the state and enable them to participate actively in the social, cultural, and political life of their society.*

| Code | Sub-indicator | Description | Topics to Identify |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **1.1** | Freedom of Belief and Religion | Right to practice, change, or abandon religion without coercion | Legal/social protections for religious practices, religious discrimination, state control over religious observances |
| **1.2** | Equality and Discrimination | Effectiveness of legal framework ensuring equal treatment | Discrimination based on: socio-economic status, gender, ethnicity, religion, foreign nationality, sexual orientation |
| **1.3** | Freedom of Expression and Opinion | Degree to which individuals can freely express opinions and criticize policies | Political opinions expression, media freedom, civil/political organization freedom, censorship, self-censorship |
| **1.4** | Freedom of Assembly and Association | Capacity to gather peacefully and form associations | CSOs, unions, political parties, harassment/interference from state, protest restrictions |
| **1.5** | Right to Life and Security of the Person | Protections against threats to physical security and life | Safety regulations, protection from violence/abuse, extrajudicial killings, torture, enforced disappearances |
| **1.6** | Freedom from Interference with Privacy | Protection from unwarranted surveillance or intrusions | Unlawful searches, wiretapping, monitoring of communications, digital surveillance |

## DIMENSION 2: Participation and Access to Information

*Mechanisms and environment that allow citizens to participate in public affairs, engage in civil society activities, and access government information.*

| Code | Sub-indicator | Description | Topics to Identify |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **2.1** | Access to Complaint Mechanisms | Availability and effectiveness of formal grievance processes | Ombudsman offices, human rights commissions, complaint channels, accountability mechanisms, trust in institutions |
| **2.2** | Non-Governmental Checks of Government Power | Role of non-governmental entities in monitoring government | Civil society organizations, media watchdogs, independent oversight, pressure/retaliation against monitors |
| **2.3** | Civic Participation | Capacity of citizens to engage in public life | Voting, political discussions, policy-making participation, right to petition, civic engagement barriers |
| **2.4** | Right to Information | Access to government-held information | Responsiveness of requests, quality of information, timeliness, affordability, general accessibility, transparency |

---

## DIMENSION 3: Labor Rights and Property Rights

*Standards and safeguards ensuring workers are treated with dignity and fairness, and protection of property rights.*

| Code | Sub-indicator | Description | Topics to Identify |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **3.1** | Labor Rights | Protection of fundamental labor rights | Equal payment and absence of discrimination, freedom to form unions and bargain collectively, prohibition of child and forced labor, working conditions, wages, migrant workers' rights |
| **3.2** | Protection from Expropriation without Due Process | Government respect for legal procedures in expropriation | Property rights for people, property rights for companies, adequate compensation, opportunity for appeal, lawful process |

---

## DIMENSION 4: Fair and Impartial Justice

*Principles ensuring all individuals have the right to seek legal redress through fair, impartial, and transparent judicial processes.*

| Code | Sub-indicator | Description | Topics to Identify |
|------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **4.1** | Access to and Affordability of Civil Justice | Ease of access to civil courts | Awareness of remedies, access to legal advice/representation, procedures, court accessibility, costs (courts, lawyers, procedures) |
| **4.2** | Improper Government Influence in Criminal Justice | Independence of criminal justice system | Interference with judiciary, prosecutors, law enforcement; political considerations in criminal cases |
| **4.3** | Discrimination in Civil Justice | Discrimination in civil justice system | Discrimination by race, gender, socioeconomic status, other characteristics; equal access to legal remedies |
| **4.4** | Improper Government Influence in Civil Justice | Independence of civil justice system | Government influence over civil court decisions, external pressures, political interests |
| **4.5** | Administrative Proceedings without Unreasonable Delay | Timeliness of administrative proceedings | Time to decision, enforcement time, delays at national/local levels, unjustified delays |
| **4.6** | Due Process of Law and Rights of the Accused | Fair trial guarantees | Presumption of innocence, arrest and pre-trial detention, torture and abusive treatment, legal assistance, rights of prisoners |
| **4.7** | Impartiality in Criminal Justice | Absence of bias in criminal justice | Police impartiality, judicial impartiality, equal treatment regardless of identity |
| **4.8** | Due Process in Administrative Proceedings | Procedural fairness in administrative matters | Right to fair hearing, access to evidence, ability to appeal, transparency in labor-related cases |

## CROSS-CUTTING TAGS

| Tag | Category | Usage |
|-----|----------|-------|
| **[CHAL]** | Challenge | Mark fragment as identifying a challenge or problem |
| **[HIGH]** | Highlight | Mark fragment as identifying progress or positive aspect |
| **[QUOTE]** | Potential Quote | Fragment suitable for direct quotation in report |
| **[QUANT]** | Quantitative Connection | Fragment that explains or contrasts with quantitative data from the WJP Index |
| **[CONTRAST]** | Expert Disagreement | Fragment where expert view differs from WJP data or other experts |

## QUANTITATIVE TRENDS TO CONTRAST (from WJP Rule of Law Index 2025)

### Key improvements to validate/explain:
- Freedom from Interference with Privacy (+32% over decade)
- Right to Life and Security (+6% over decade)
- Freedom of Belief and Religion (+10% over 5 years)
- Criminal Justice indicators at highest levels in decade

### Key declines to explain:
- Equality and Discrimination (-2% last year)
- Freedom of Expression and Opinion (-4% last year)
- All Participation and Access to Information indicators declining
- Labor Rights (-5% from 2020-2025)
- Protection from Expropriation (-6% from 2020-2025)
- Civil Justice indicators at lowest levels of decade
