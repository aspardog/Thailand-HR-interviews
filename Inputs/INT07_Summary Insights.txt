Civil & Political Rights
* Civic space has improved compared to the immediate post-coup period, but political expression remains constrained by legal risk and selective enforcement, namely in cases linked to the monarchy. Public debate and participation have expanded over the past several years. Parliamentary processes function more normally than during the period of military rule, and discussion of social and policy issues has become more visible. At the same time, legal exposure continues to limit political speech. Section 112 (lese-majeste) remains a central constraint. Cases continue to be filed and prosecuted, and the law is broadly interpreted in practice. The evidentiary threshold in some cases is viewed as low, namely in online speech cases, which increases uncertainty and discourages open political discussion. Reform of this provision is widely regarded as politically difficult.
* Freedom of assembly faces similar pressures. Demonstrations often proceed without immediate interference, but participants may subsequently receive summonses or charges, which leads to ongoing legal and financial burdens for organizers and protest networks. At the same time, local civic activity has increased in many provinces, with communities outside Bangkok organizing more frequently around land, environmental, and development concerns. Proposed or discussed regulatory measures for civil society organizations, including additional reporting and compliance requirements, have raised concerns about administrative control and selective scrutiny of politically active groups.
Participation and access to information 
* Formal transparency has increased through digitization, but practical access to information remains uneven and dependent on capacity and resources. Government documents and public information are more widely available online than a decade ago. Individuals who know where to search and how to navigate official systems can obtain relevant materials more easily. However, effective access isn't uniform. Communities affected by development or environmental projects often lack the tools or skills to locate and interpret lengthy technical documents. In some cases, obtaining full reports involves cost or administrative barriers. As a result, formal publication doesn't always translate into meaningful participation.
* Political participation has increased since the protest movements of 2020-2021, including more frequent local mobilization around land use and development concerns. At the same time, legal and administrative pressures on civil society risk limiting sustained engagement.
* More broadly, complaint mechanisms for discrimination and rights violations are limited in accessibility and effectiveness. Existing laws often emphasize policy commitments instead of clear, practical pathways for individuals to seek redress. This gap reduces confidence in equal treatment before the law.3


Labor and property rights
* Labor protections are comparatively well established in law, but vulnerable groups face structural barriers that limit equal access to employment and remedies. Statutory protections have expanded in recent years, including improvements in leave entitlements and formal safeguards. Many workers report the ability to organize and negotiate working conditions.
* In practice, outcomes differ significantly across groups. Migrant workers remain exposed to precarious conditions and limited access to services. Additional discrimination affects people with disabilities, people living with HIV, transgender individuals, and people with criminal records, who may encounter exclusion through screening practices or informal barriers. These factors reduce the practical effectiveness of formal labor protections.
Fair & Impartial Justice
* Legal frameworks addressing abuse and discrimination are in place, but enforcement and accountability remain slow and inconsistent. Mechanisms exist to address torture, enforced disappearance, and related violations, and some cases have reached the courts. Progress remains limited, however, due to slow investigations and weak accountability, particularly where state actors are implicated. Families of victims face difficulties securing remedies.
