# Prompt: Chapter 2 Draft Generation (Step 3B)

You are writing Chapter 2 of the Thailand Human Rights Report 2025: the qualitative analysis chapter based on expert interviews.

## Inputs You Will Receive:
1. **ALL Dimension Synthesis files** (DIMENSION_#_*.md)
2. **QUOTE_BANK.md**
3. **CODEBOOK.md** for framework reference

## Your Task:

Write a narrative chapter that presents qualitative findings in an academic/policy report style.

### Chapter Structure:

```
# Chapter 2: Qualitative Analysis - Expert Perspectives on Human Rights in Thailand

## Introduction
[1 paragraph: methodology overview - 13 experts, 9 interviews, WJP framework]

## Section 2.0: Overall Assessment
[3-4 paragraphs synthesizing Dimension 0]
- Expert consensus
- The selective enforcement paradox
- Geographic variation (Deep South)
- Progress amid constraints

## Section 2.1: Civil and Political Rights
[4-5 paragraphs synthesizing Dimension 1]
- Freedom of Expression: Section 112
- Freedom of Assembly
- Right to Life and Security: Impunity
- Privacy: PDPA progress with caveats

## Section 2.2: Participation and Access to Information
[3-4 paragraphs synthesizing Dimension 2]
- Constitutional Court power
- Access to information gaps
- Public participation limitations
- Youth engagement

## Section 2.3: Labor Rights and Property Rights
[3-4 paragraphs synthesizing Dimension 3]
- Two-tier labor system
- Fishing industry crisis
- Property enforcement failures
- Progress areas

## Section 2.4: Fair and Impartial Justice
[4-5 paragraphs synthesizing Dimension 4]
- Two systems of justice
- Deep South exception
- Access barriers (cost, duration)
- SLAPP suits and over-criminalization
```

### Writing Style:
- **Academic but accessible**: Suitable for policy audience
- **Evidence-based**: Every claim supported by expert quote
- **Balanced**: Present both challenges and progress
- **Analytical**: Explain WHY, not just describe WHAT
- **Connected**: Link qualitative findings to quantitative trends

### Quote Integration:
- Use 2-3 blockquotes per section
- Introduce quotes with context
- Follow quotes with analysis
- Format:
```
> "Quote text here."
> — Expert Type (INT##)
```

### Paragraph Structure:
1. Topic sentence (claim)
2. Evidence (expert observations, quotes)
3. Analysis (what this means, why it matters)
4. Connection (to broader patterns or quantitative data)

## Critical Rules:
- NO information that isn't in the dimension syntheses or quote bank
- Every major claim needs a supporting quote
- Maintain consistent voice throughout
- Use transitions between sections
- End each section with forward-looking observation or synthesis

## Output Format:
Markdown document ready for conversion to final report format.
