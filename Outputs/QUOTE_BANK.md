## 1.3 Freedom of Expression

> **"One of the issues that Thai people never ever been able to speak up publicly is about the issue of the monarchy."**
> — INT02 (UNDP Representatives) | `INT02.txt:178`

> **"We have people going to jail because of the issue every single month."**
> — INT09 (Civic Tech Expert) | `INT09.txt:214`

> **"Almost 2000 people have been charged or prosecuted under various repressive laws."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:19`

> **"The number of political cases skyrocketed from 30 to 40 cases per year to 800 cases by the end of 2021."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:17`

> **"In general, when it comes to freedom of expression, it's quite all right unless and until you start speaking about quite controversial issues... like les majestic, like monarchy reforms, like the military."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:94`

> **"When it comes to political topics, this is when you see people start to either just walk away completely because they see no point in the risk of sharing certain political opinions."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:180`

> **"Even we have a good counterpart at the Thai Ministry of Justice, it's still something that they are not actually able to say it or to work on it... it will be like a dead end kind of things."**
> — INT07 (International HR Expert) | `INT07.txt:88`

> **"42 or 44 of them are likely to be prosecuted for the fact that they propose a bill that would curb the enforcement of Les Majeste law."**
> — INT04 (Academic) | `INT04.txt:131`

> **"You have this Computer Crime Act which is increasingly being used and you can be held accountable for something you say, something you repost and face criminal charges and jail time."**
> — INT02 (UNDP Representatives) | `INT02.txt:131`

> **"Just only the business related SLAPP action we found that nearly 100 cases so far... if you would like to also collect the data of the SLAPP practice of the government authority, it could be up to 1000 cases."**
> — INT02 (UNDP Representatives) | `INT02.txt:143`

> **"Under Thai legal regime, forum shopping is allowed. So even though the person being targeted has primary residence in Bangkok, they can file a lawsuit as far away as the northernmost province."**
> — INT04 (Academic) | `INT04.txt:327`

> **"When it comes to the indictment rate for les majestic cases, I think it's around ninety-eight percent, ninety-nine percent."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:313`

# DIMENSION 3: Labor Rights and Property Rights

## 3.1 Labor Rights

> **"Migrant workers can't form their own unions... that's one of the big problems... at most they could just join some other union."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:141`

> **"Thailand also sees the migrant worker as a threat to national security... that's why they never ratified the ILO convention."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:167`

> **"The biggest problem now would be the undocumented migrant workers who almost have no access to anything... We have a lot here... It's like million to million."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:196`

> **"You hear stories of 50 boats going out, two weeks later coming in at 30 or 40 people."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:259`

> **"Laborers are being brought in directly from neighboring countries illegally through brokers where they pay the brokers, they take on a large debt to pay the brokers."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:254`

> **"The informal sector. The numbers of these workers in this sector now accounts for over 50% of the total workers."**
> — INT02 (UNDP Representatives) | `INT02.txt:272`

> **"When they submit an application to form the unions, the authority will check with their employer... the employer knows and when they know they will start to use all the tactics to stop these people from forming unions."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:145`

> **"Labour court is free... They have lawyers who stationed there to provide free service... in general labour court is OK."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:229`

> **"For the labor court, I think that it acts really independently. No one can put pressure on the labor court."**
> — INT03 (Employment Lawyer) | `INT03.txt:303`

> **"The labour situation in Thailand is more developing and already some is mature if compared to 20 years ago."**
> — INT03 (Employment Lawyer) | `INT03.txt:44`

> **"Back in the days you'll see construction sites, guys wearing flip-flops, shirtless, with no head protection. Those are the days of the past and these are strictly enforced now."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:297`

## 3.2 Property Rights

> **"We won the case. It was an obvious case of fraud. But the guy just doesn't pay out and to this day there's really nothing we could have done."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:348`

> **"In Thailand, if the guy doesn't pay and he goes into hiding and if he's tricky, what he does is he moves all his money or his assets out of his own name, puts it into his friend's name or his family member's name."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:348`

> **"Prior to PDPA you would be able to walk in with a court judgment and say, hey, can you check what bank accounts or how much money this person has? Banks nowadays argue data privacy concerns even with a court judgment."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:87`

> **"My client ended up losing a few million dollars just because the buyer had disputed their claim... under Thai civil procedures, unless and until that case was basically finished in court... the boat itself couldn't leave port."**

# DIMENSION 4: Fair and Impartial Justice

## 4.2 Government Influence

> **"2019 Supreme Court regulation that requires judges to submit drafts order or draft judgments to be reviewed by the regional Chief Justice before those judgments or orders can be issued. So again, where's the independence there?"**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:324`

> **"It's very common for judges to take a 5 minute recess, 10 minute recess, 30 minute recess to walk out of the courtroom to consult the court executives about what to do in their case."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:325`

> **"Under Thai law they allow the senior judge to review the draft judgment of the junior judges... they feel like they're under pressure to change the draft judgment to the way that senior recommended."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:320`

> **"The Constitutional Court, which has proved time and again that they don't really work to protect the rights enshrined in the Constitution. They're not the guardian of the Constitution. Instead, they're being used as a political tool."**
> — INT04 (Academic) | `INT04.txt:237`

> **"Just a small group of people can kind of demolish everything we did during that big day."**
> — INT09 (Civic Tech Expert) | `INT09.txt:420`

> **"It kind of shift from a warfare to a lawfare context... there may not be a reason or need for a coup because you can play out whatever needs to be played out through the court system."**
> — INT02 (UNDP Representatives) | `INT02.txt:190`

## 4.4 Impartiality

> **"I think it's worse. I think it's cultural... we're taught to respect our elders, our seniors... the relationship, the seniority is at play here."**
> — INT04 (Academic) | `INT04.txt:544`

> **"If you're someone of influence or if you know someone or if you have connections with certain agencies, your matter or your case will go a lot quicker than the average Joe."**
> — INT08 (Commercial Lawyer) | `INT08.txt:404`

> **"The rich people did serious issues... they drove drunk, crashed people, killed people, but a lot of them... the case delayed and finally people don't get any information."**
> — INT09 (Civic Tech Expert) | `INT09.txt:424`

> **"Victim recognition and access to remedies remain inconsistent. Compensation and accountability mechanisms are seen as favoring state-affiliated or majority communities, while Malay Muslim victims face greater barriers to redress."**
> — INT01 (Deep South HR Defender) | `INT01.txt:277`

## 4.6 Due Process

> **"Last year alone we've seen quite a number of secret trial orders being issued to kick members of the public out of the courtroom."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:329`

> **"The persistent refusal of judges to issue subpoenas for the defense counsel... judges would not issue subpoenas in any of these cases. They would say, well, it's about the king, it's national security."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:333`

> **"The issue of right to bail. Many people are put in detention pending trial or pending appeal. So again, that's a clear violation of the right to presumption of innocence."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:338`

> **"We in Thailand, we use criminal law too much. A lot of things are criminal offenses even though they should not be."**
> — INT03 (Employment Lawyer) | `INT03.txt:425`

> **"They even introduced the law that allow the so-called anti-SLAPP provisions... But the judges haven't exercised those powers at all."**
> — INT05 (HR NGO Representative) | `INT05.txt:281`

## 4.5 Timeliness

> **"When discussed about the case going to the trial part, for sure one case should be about 8 months to 10 months."**
> — INT03 (Employment Lawyer) | `INT03.txt:337`

> **"The administrative court has emerged as one of the more reliable mechanisms... They average about a year and a half to two years."**
> — INT04 (Academic) | `INT04.txt:217`

> **"A lot of people try to withdraw their appealing at some stage because they don't have the money, time, resource to fight anymore."**
> — INT09 (Civic Tech Expert) | `INT09.txt:430`

## 4.7 Impartiality in Criminal Justice

> **"We didn't see the impact in terms of accountability to the state... the states do have impunity from things actually on torture and enforced disappearance."**
> — INT07 (International HR Expert) | `INT07.txt:390`

> **"Only the trainer will be pinned out and things doesn't move anywhere within the military. They still do the same practice."**
> — INT07 (International HR Expert) | `INT07.txt:400`

## 4.8 Correctional System

> **"Access to healthcare, that's going to be an issue. There's a women's prison whereby there's a number of pills that one prison cell can get access to per day."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:447`

> **"When you take a shower, that would count from 1 to 15. And if you don't finish by then, you need to finish by 15 seconds."**
> — INT06 (Thai Lawyers for Human Rights) | `INT06.txt:448`
