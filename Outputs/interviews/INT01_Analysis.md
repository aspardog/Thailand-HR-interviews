## Executive Summary

This expert provides critical testimony on the human rights situation in Thailand's Deep South (Pattani, Yala, Narathiwat), where a 20-year insurgency has resulted in a distinct legal regime governed by special security laws. The interview reveals systematic discrimination against Malay Muslims, severe constraints on civic space, documented torture cases, and a two-tiered justice system where outcomes depend on ethnicity. Despite reduced casualties, the expert emphasizes that people feel neither safer nor more free due to pervasive surveillance and intimidation.

## Key Challenges [CHAL]

### 1. Special Security Laws Regime
**Dimension Code:** 1.5, 4.6
**Sub-indicator:** Right to Life and Security; Due Process

**Description:** Three overlapping special laws (Martial Law, Emergency Decree, Internal Security Act) have been in effect for over 22 years, allowing detention without warrant for up to 30 days in military facilities where torture has been documented.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "We have special law, not only the criminal law or civil law... One is martial law, the 2nd is emergency. And the third is Internal Security Act, and the martial law can detain people without arrested violence in seven days. And then they continue to using, uh, the emergency degree that they can detain, uh, people without Alicia Boland. 28 day..."

> "...they can detain people without arrested violent one month and during the detention they're not detained in the prison, they detain special detentions based in military camp and police camp and we found since 2004 until now they have like a torture case inside this detention and some of them were die during interrogation and also someone die during a transportation."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM (Malay Muslims), VG-HR (Human Rights Defenders)
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS

---

### 2. Two-Tiered Justice System
**Dimension Code:** 4.7, 4.3
**Sub-indicator:** Impartiality in Criminal Justice; Discrimination in Civil Justice

**Description:** Justice outcomes systematically differ based on ethnicity. When Malay Muslims are victims, perpetrators are not found; when they are accused, prosecution is swift and sentences severe.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "When the Malayu Muslim are victim, they're not finding the perpetrator. Because they think that who die from shooting are related with a member of the non-state armed group, but in the same families who are who was arrested. The process that bring them to the court is quickly, very quickly and the sentence like a life sentence or execution."

> "When we think about the compensation to the victims who die from the conflict, they only support the victim who are authority or Buddhist only and they deny to compensate to the Malayu Muslim side even though they are government officer or not."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS

### 3. Surveillance and Chilling Effect on Civic Space
**Dimension Code:** 1.6, 1.3, 1.4
**Sub-indicator:** Privacy; Freedom of Expression; Freedom of Assembly

**Description:** Pervasive surveillance of activists and civil society, combined with criminal prosecution for social media activity, creates a chilling effect that suppresses participation even without formal prohibitions.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "...in three years we have 22 activists and human rights defenders shot by the military department and the police due to posting or have SMB in the public space..."

> "...we have in 2022 we have the big... organized by the human rights defenders and activists in the Deep South who are minority group... 50,000 Malayu Muslim, not only men but women also, joining this assembly were surveillance by the military after that. By searching at their house because they have picture. They take a picture all people who joining the SMB and they follow them to the to their house..."

> "...they change their behaviour. They cannot presenting about the our brave or presenting the situation because they don't know what happened after that..."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM, VG-HR, VG-PA
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS

### 4. Compromised Judicial Independence
**Dimension Code:** 4.2, 4.4
**Sub-indicator:** Improper Government Influence in Criminal/Civil Justice

**Description:** Police and prosecutors operate under military control in national security cases. Evidence of judicial pressure exists, including a judge suicide case linked to pressure to change sentences.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "...police and prosecutor is not independent. They're under the military during the interrogation process. They're using the evidence from the interrogation process in the military camp..."

> "...we have case of the judge suicide in the court due to the leader of the judge order him to change the sentence. In case of national security case."

> "...the justice process in the Deep South in case of national security under the military, not under the chart."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS

### 5. Ineffective Complaint Mechanisms
**Dimension Code:** 2.1
**Sub-indicator:** Access to Complaint Mechanisms

**Description:** National Human Rights Council has lost credibility due to military officer appointments and failure to hold security forces accountable. Parliamentary referrals produce no local change.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "...we cannot believe about the National Security Council, National Human Rights Council. Because of last three years, one military changed their position to apply to be officer in the National Human Rights Council..."

> "...we found that after the the claim or the people, the victim came to this sector. They mostly not have any action. They only said that this is the right of the military, this is the discipline of the military..."

> "...some case in the South sending to the parliament, but like a committee... they will send the case to the parliament and investigation in the committee. But it's not like it's not happen anything better."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM, VG-HR
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS

### 6. Systematic Discrimination Against Malay Muslims
**Dimension Code:** 1.2
**Sub-indicator:** Equality and Discrimination

**Description:** Deep-rooted discrimination based on ethnic and religious identity, where Malay Muslims are stigmatized as potential insurgents regardless of their actions.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "...the center of the country or the government of the country think that the Malayu Muslim here are member of the movement group. This is a stigmatization and discrimination against the Malayu Muslim..."

> "...they think that this is the the second class of the country they can control... they're using torture and enforced to disappearance people and killing and bombing people."

> "...in the Bangkok they focus on the 112 or monarchy... But in this Deep South we have... when the youth or boy assembly together. They think that this is the people want to separate from the country."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-MM
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-DS
