## Executive Summary

This expert from Thai Lawyers for Human Rights provides the most detailed quantitative and qualitative data on political prosecution and criminal justice failures. Key statistics: cases rose from 30-40/year (2014-2019) to 800 by end of 2021; nearly 2000 people charged since 2020; Les Majeste indictment rate is 98-99%. The expert characterizes the situation as: **"Human rights situation looks good, but it's a lot worse than it looks."** After apparent return to civilian government, political prosecution continues, judgments from backlog are now being handed down, and protest leaders are fleeing Thailand. The interview documents extensive judicial independence problems: senior judges review junior judges' drafts, judges consult executives mid-hearing, secret trials, gag orders, denied defense subpoenas, and bail refusals. Political prisoners face poor conditions.

## Key Challenges [CHAL]

### 1. Massive Scale of Political Prosecution
**Dimension Code:** 1.3, 4.7
**Sub-indicator:** Freedom of Expression; Impartiality in Criminal Justice

**Description:** Political prosecutions exploded after 2020 protests: from 30-40 cases/year to 800 by 2021, with ~2000 people charged under various repressive laws. New cases continue under civilian government.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "Between 2014 and 2019... we represented around 30 to 40 cases per year, cases of political cases... Then came the 2020 movement... and the number of political cases skyrocketed from 30 to 40 cases per year to 800 cases by the end of 2021..."

> "...almost 2000 people have been charged or prosecuted under various repressive laws, emergency decree... Las Majestas, Sedition, Public Assembly Act, Committed Crimes Act..."

> "...with the return of the civilian LED government after the 2023 general election... political prosecution unfortunately has not disappeared... we have seen more than 100 new cases..."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-PA, VG-YT, VG-HR
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-NA

### 3. Near-100% Les Majeste Indictment Rate
**Dimension Code:** 4.7
**Sub-indicator:** Impartiality in Criminal Justice

**Description:** Les Majeste cases have 98-99% indictment rate, suggesting prosecutorial policy rather than merit-based review. Enforcement varies with political direction.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "...when it comes to less majestic cases, I think the indictment rate is around ninety-eight percent, ninety-nine percent. It was 100% before... that seems to me that there's some sort of policy there at the Attorney General office..."

> "...back in 2018 to 2020, there were zero Les Majeste cases because the Prime Minister back then said the king said we shouldn't be using this law... And then in 2020... the Prime Minister came out and say again... It's time to bring back the law... And then suddenly we see a spike..."

**Vulnerable Groups:** VG-PA
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-NA

### 4. Senior Judges Review Junior Judges' Draft Judgments
**Dimension Code:** 4.2, 4.4
**Sub-indicator:** Improper Government Influence; Impartiality

**Description:** 2019 Supreme Court regulation requires judges to submit draft orders/judgments to regional Chief Justice for review before issuance. Judges routinely consult executives mid-hearing.

**Supporting Evidence:**
> "2019 Supreme Court regulation that requires judges to submit drafts order or draft judgments to be reviewed by the regional Chief Justice before those judgments or orders can be issued. So again, where's the independence there?"

> "...it's very common for judges to take a 5 minute recess, 10 minute recess, 30 minute recess to walk out of the courtroom to consult the court executives about what to do in their case, about whether to grant bail, about whether to postpone a case about whether to issue a subpoena for the defense..."

**Vulnerable Groups:** -
**Geographic Scope:** GEO-NA
