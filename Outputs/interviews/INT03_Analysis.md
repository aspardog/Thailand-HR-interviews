## Executive Summary

This expert provides an employer-side perspective on labor rights and justice in Thailand. The interview reveals a nuanced view where formal legal frameworks function adequately but practical outcomes depend heavily on economic conditions, access to quality legal representation, and systemic factors like delays and costs. The expert sees overall improvement in human rights ("getting improved") but highlights concerns about unions abusing legal mechanisms to pressure employers, over-criminalization of regulatory violations, and the gap between formal transparency and substantive accountability.
