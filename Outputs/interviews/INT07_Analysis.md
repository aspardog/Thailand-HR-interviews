## Executive Summary

This international human rights expert provides a nuanced assessment recognizing improvement since the coup while identifying structural constraints. Section 112 (lese-majeste) is characterized as "dead end" for reform - even working with government counterparts, this issue cannot be discussed. Key themes include: delayed enforcement creating uncertainty (protests proceed but charges come later), uneven digitalization that publishes information but doesn't ensure accessible participation, and discrimination affecting multiple vulnerable groups (migrants, indigenous people, people with disabilities, HIV+ individuals, drug offenders, transgender persons). The expert highlights that torture/enforced disappearance laws exist but state impunity persists for cases involving powerful actors. The overall trajectory: "improved right now" but "downhill trend."
