## Executive Summary

This commercial lawyer provides a business-oriented perspective that captures a crucial insight: **"Fundamental rights are there, but enforcement is usually cherry-picked."** The expert describes Thailand as progressive on social issues (same-sex marriage) but politically restrictive, with Les Majeste and the Computer Crime Act used selectively against critics. The interview documents significant enforcement problems: banks refusing to cooperate with court judgments citing PDPA privacy concerns, fraudsters hiding assets in family members' names with impunity, and procedural inefficiencies causing multi-million dollar losses. Labor tribunals are positively assessed as pro-employee and efficient, but migrant workers in fisheries face severe abuses including debt bondage and deaths at sea. The expert emphasizes Thailand's relationship-based culture creates uneven treatment: connected individuals get 48-hour turnaround while others wait 60 days.
